Meet Mariah

A one-woman bakery, a wild starter, and a stubborn belief that bread should be made of bread.

The story

I started baking sourdough a few years ago after I couldn't find a single loaf at the grocery store that didn't list ingredients I didn't recognize. I figured I'd try making my own. One loaf turned into two, then ten, then a wait list of friends and neighbors asking for more.

Mariah's Sourdough Co. grew out of that — quietly, one bake at a time. Today every loaf is still shaped, scored, and baked by me.

What goes into the bread

Three ingredients. That's the whole list:

  • Certified-organic flour from a small regional mill.
  • Filtered water.
  • Hand-harvested sea salt.

That's it. No commercial yeast, no dough conditioners, no preservatives, no enrichers, no oils, no sugars (unless the recipe specifically calls for it, like the cinnamon raisin). The fermentation does all the work — and it takes its time.

Why I do it this way

Real bread used to be a staple — something that nourished a family for the week. Somewhere along the way it became fluffy, fast, and full of things our bodies don't recognize. I bake the way bakers have for thousands of years because that's the version of bread your body actually knows how to use. And honestly? It just tastes better.
